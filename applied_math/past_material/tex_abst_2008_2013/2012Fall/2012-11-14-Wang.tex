\newcommand{\talktitle}{A Simple Proof\\ of the Hartman-Grobman Theorem}

\newcommand{\talkspeaker}{ {\sc Alex Wang}\\ \textit{Texas Tech University}}

\newcommand{\talkdate}{Wednesday, November 14, 2012}

\newcommand{\timelocation}{Room: MATH 014.  Time: 4:00pm.}

\newcommand{\talkabstract}{
Hartman-Grobman Theorem states that a differential equation
$du/dt=f(u)$ can be changed into a linear system in a neighborhood
of a hyperbolic equilibrium point by applying a nonlinear
coordinate change. In this talk, we will give a simple proof of
the Hartman-Grobman theorem.
}
